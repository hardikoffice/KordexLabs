# AI Stock Analysis: Meta and Alphabet Gain Momentum as Microsoft Faces Short-Term Headwinds

In the rapidly evolving landscape of high-growth technology, performing a regular **AI stock analysis** is essential for investors looking to capitalize on the next wave of cloud and intelligence-driven revenue. The last 24 hours have highlighted a distinct divergence among the "Magnificent Seven," specifically between Microsoft (MSFT), Meta Platforms (META), and Alphabet (GOOGL). While the broader market remains cautiously optimistic, individual segment performance in cloud infrastructure and generative AI integration is now the primary driver of valuation.

## Meta and Alphabet: Leading the AI Charge

Alphabet (GOOGL) and Meta (META) showed resilience in the most recent trading session. Meta Platforms rose by 1.03%, closing at $654.07, signaling continued investor confidence in its AI-driven advertising efficiency and the long-term potential of its hardware ecosystem. Alphabet followed suit with a modest gain of 0.22%, closing at $307.04.

Looking back at the trajectory into 2026, Alphabet has significantly outperformed its peers. With a staggering 65% gain in 2025, Google Cloud has become the company's primary growth engine. Last quarter, Google Cloud revenue soared by 34%, while its segment operating income surged by 84%. This growth is fueled by massive adoption of [AI tools](/tools) such as the Vertex AI platform and Gemini Enterprise integrations.

## Microsoft: Navigating a Consolidation Phase

Microsoft (MSFT) faced a slight pullback in the last 24 hours, dropping 0.89% to close at $405.76. Despite this short-term dip, the fundamentals of the Redmond-based giant remain robust. Microsoft reported an 18% year-over-year revenue increase and a 23% jump in adjusted earnings per share (EPS).

However, in our recent [stock analysis](/markets), we noted that Microsoft’s stock performance has slightly trailed the S&P 500 recently. This is largely attributed to the market pricing in its early lead with OpenAI and shifting focus toward how the company will sustain its margins amidst rising infrastructure costs. For investors, the current consolidation may represent a strategic entry point before the next phase of Azure AI scaling.
